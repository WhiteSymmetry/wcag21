Understanding Adapting text

Intent

The intent of this Success Criterion is to help ensure that people with low vision who override spacing or font family (also called font face or typeface) can read text. Some people with disabilities override author settings via user stylesheet, bookmarklet, extension, or application. A user may need to change font family to effectively read text. They may increase spacing between lines (also called leading), words, and letters to effectively read text. Line spacing also helps with tracking. Tracking is following along lines of text, including getting from the end of one line to the beginning of the next line of text.

For this Success Criterion the canvas implementation of text is considered to be images of text.

Benefits

  • People with low vision who require increased space between lines, words and letters are to able to read text.
  • People with low vision have clickable links, when a large font-family is selected and/or increased spacing is applied to text.

Examples

  • Text does not wrap out of the containing box or cause boxes to overlap when a large font-family is selected and/or increased spacing is applied to text.
  • Links are operable when a large font-family is selected and/or increased spacing is applied to text.
